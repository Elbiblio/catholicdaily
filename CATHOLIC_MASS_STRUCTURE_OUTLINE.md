# Catholic Mass Structure - Complete Outline

This document provides a comprehensive outline of the Ordinary Form of the Roman Rite (Novus Ordo) Mass, including all prayers, priest dialogues, congregational responses, and readings in their correct liturgical order.

---

## I. INTRODUCTORY RITES

### 1. Entrance Procession
- **Priest and ministers enter** in procession
- **Entrance Antiphon** (sung or recited) - varies by day

### 2. Greeting
- **Priest:** "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit."
- **People:** "Amen."
- **Priest:** "The Lord be with you."
- **People:** "And with your spirit."
- *(Alternative greeting formulas exist)*

### 3. Penitential Act (Forms A, B, or C)

#### Form A: Confiteor
- **All:** "I confess to almighty God and to you, my brothers and sisters, that I have greatly sinned in my thoughts and in my words, in what I have done and in what I have failed to do, through my fault, through my fault, through my most grievous fault; therefore I ask blessed Mary ever-Virgin, all the Angels and Saints, and you, my brothers and sisters, to pray for me to the Lord our God."
- **Priest:** "May almighty God have mercy on us, forgive us our sins, and bring us to everlasting life."
- **People:** "Amen."

#### Form B: "Have mercy on us, Lord"
- **Priest:** "Have mercy on us, Lord."
- **People:** "For we have sinned against you."
- **Priest:** "Show us, O Lord, your mercy."
- **People:** "And grant us your salvation."

#### Form C: Kyrie
- **Priest/People:** "Lord, have mercy." / "Lord, have mercy."
- **Priest/People:** "Christ, have mercy." / "Christ, have mercy."
- **Priest/People:** "Lord, have mercy." / "Lord, have mercy."

*(Followed by)*
- **Priest:** "May almighty God have mercy on us, forgive us our sins, and bring us to everlasting life."
- **People:** "Amen."

### 4. Kyrie Eleison (if not used in Form C)
- **Priest/People:** "Lord, have mercy." (repeated)
- **Priest/People:** "Christ, have mercy." (repeated)
- **Priest/People:** "Lord, have mercy." (repeated)

### 5. Gloria (Sundays, Solemnities, and Feasts - omitted during Advent and Lent)
- **All:** "Glory to God in the highest, and on earth peace to people of good will. We praise you, we bless you, we adore you, we glorify you, we give you thanks for your great glory, Lord God, heavenly King, O God, almighty Father. Lord Jesus Christ, Only Begotten Son, Lord God, Lamb of God, Son of the Father, you take away the sins of the world, have mercy on us; you take away the sins of the world, receive our prayer; you are seated at the right hand of the Father, have mercy on us. For you alone are the Holy One, you alone are the Lord, you alone are the Most High, Jesus Christ, with the Holy Spirit, in the glory of God the Father. Amen."

### 6. Collect (Opening Prayer)
- **Priest:** "Let us pray." (followed by silence)
- **Priest:** [Collect prayer - varies by day]
- **People:** "Amen."

---

## II. LITURGY OF THE WORD

### 7. First Reading
- **Reader proclaims** the First Reading from the Old Testament (or Acts during Easter season)
- **Reader:** "The Word of the Lord."
- **People:** "Thanks be to God."

### 8. Responsorial Psalm
- **Psalmist or cantor** sings or recites the psalm response
- **People** respond with the antiphon/refrain after each verse
- *(The psalm is between the first and second readings)*

### 9. Second Reading (Sundays and Solemnities only)
- **Reader proclaims** the Second Reading from the Epistles or other New Testament writings
- **Reader:** "The Word of the Lord."
- **People:** "Thanks be to God."

### 10. Gospel Acclamation (Alleluia)
- **Cantor/People:** "Alleluia, alleluia." (or alternate during Lent)
- **Verse** is sung or recited
- **People:** "Alleluia." (repeated)

### 11. Gospel Reading
- **Deacon or Priest:** "The Lord be with you."
- **People:** "And with your spirit."
- **Deacon or Priest:** "A reading from the holy Gospel according to [N]."
- **People:** "Glory to you, O Lord."
- *(All make the sign of the cross on forehead, lips, and heart)*
- **Deacon or Priest** proclaims the Gospel
- **Deacon or Priest:** "The Gospel of the Lord."
- **People:** "Praise to you, Lord Jesus Christ."

### 12. Homily
- **Priest or Deacon** preaches on the readings and applies them to daily life
- *(Sit during homily)*

### 13. Creed (Sundays and Solemnities)

#### Nicene Creed
- **All:** "I believe in one God, the Father almighty, maker of heaven and earth, of all things visible and invisible. And in one Lord Jesus Christ, the Only Begotten Son of God, born of the Father before all ages. God from God, Light from Light, true God from true God, begotten, not made, consubstantial with the Father; through him all things were made. For us men and for our salvation he came down from heaven, and by the Holy Spirit was incarnate of the Virgin Mary, and became man. For our sake he was crucified under Pontius Pilate, he suffered death and was buried, and rose again on the third day in accordance with the Scriptures. He ascended into heaven and is seated at the right hand of the Father. He will come again in glory to judge the living and the dead and his kingdom will have no end. I believe in the Holy Spirit, the Lord, the giver of life, who proceeds from the Father and the Son, who with the Father and the Son is adored and glorified, who has spoken through the prophets. I believe in one, holy, catholic and apostolic Church. I confess one baptism for the forgiveness of sins and I look forward to the resurrection of the dead and the life of the world to come. Amen."

#### Apostles' Creed (may be used, especially in Masses with children)
- **All:** "I believe in God, the Father almighty, Creator of heaven and earth, and in Jesus Christ, his only Son, our Lord, who was conceived by the Holy Spirit, born of the Virgin Mary, suffered under Pontius Pilate, was crucified, died and was buried; he descended into hell; on the third day he rose again from the dead; he ascended into heaven, and is seated at the right hand of God the Father almighty; from there he will come to judge the living and the dead. I believe in the Holy Spirit, the holy Catholic Church, the communion of saints, the forgiveness of sins, the resurrection of the body, and life everlasting. Amen."

### 14. Prayer of the Faithful (Universal Prayer)
- **Priest:** "Let us pray for..." (introduces each intention)
- **Deacon or Lector** reads each intention
- **People respond** after each intention: "Lord, hear our prayer." or similar
- **Priest** concludes with a prayer
- **People:** "Amen."

---

## III. LITURGY OF THE EUCHARIST

### 15. Presentation of the Gifts
- **Faithful bring forward** the bread, wine, and offerings
- **Priest receives** the gifts and places them on the altar
- **Priest (quietly):** "Blessed are you, Lord God of all creation, for through your goodness we have received the bread we offer you: fruit of the earth and work of human hands, it will become for us the bread of life."
- **People:** "Blessed be God for ever."
- **Priest (quietly):** "Blessed are you, Lord God of all creation, for through your goodness we have received the wine we offer you: fruit of the vine and work of human hands, it will become our spiritual drink."
- **People:** "Blessed be God for ever."
- **Priest washes his hands** (quietly): "Wash me, O Lord, from my iniquity and cleanse me from my sin."
- **Priest:** "Pray, brothers and sisters, that my sacrifice and yours may be acceptable to God, the almighty Father."
- **People:** "May the Lord accept the sacrifice at your hands for the praise and glory of his name, for our good and the good of all his holy Church."

### 16. Prayer over the Offerings
- **Priest:** "Pray, brethren..." (or similar invitation)
- **Priest** prays the Prayer over the Offerings (varies by day)
- **People:** "Amen."

### 17. Eucharistic Prayer

#### Preface Dialogue
- **Priest:** "The Lord be with you."
- **People:** "And with your spirit."
- **Priest:** "Lift up your hearts."
- **People:** "We lift them up to the Lord."
- **Priest:** "Let us give thanks to the Lord our God."
- **People:** "It is right and just."

#### Preface
- **Priest** recites or sings the Preface (varies by season/occasion)

#### Sanctus
- **All:** "Holy, Holy, Holy Lord God of hosts. Heaven and earth are full of your glory. Hosanna in the highest. Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord. Hosanna in the highest."

#### Epiclesis (Invocation of the Holy Spirit)
- **Priest** extends hands over the gifts and calls down the Holy Spirit

#### Institution Narrative and Consecration
- **Priest** takes the bread: "Take this, all of you, and eat of it, for this is my body, which will be given up for you."
- **Priest** elevates the consecrated host - **People kneel**
- **Priest** takes the chalice: "Take this, all of you, and drink from it, for this is the chalice of my blood, the blood of the new and eternal covenant, which will be poured out for you and for many for the forgiveness of sins. Do this in memory of me."
- **Priest** elevates the chalice - **People kneel**

#### Acclamation after Consecration
- **Priest:** "The mystery of faith."
- **People** (choose one):
  - "We proclaim your Death, O Lord, and profess your Resurrection until you come again."
  - OR "When we eat this Bread and drink this Cup, we proclaim your Death, O Lord, until you come again."
  - OR "Save us, Savior of the world, for by your Cross and Resurrection you have set us free."

#### Anamnesis (Memorial)
- **Priest** continues the Eucharistic Prayer

#### Intercessions
- **Priest** prays for the living and dead, the Church, etc.

#### Final Doxology
- **Priest** elevates host and chalice: "Through him, and with him, and in him, O God, almighty Father, in the unity of the Holy Spirit, all glory and honor is yours, for ever and ever."
- **People:** "Amen." (Great Amen)

### 18. The Lord's Prayer
- **Priest:** "At the Savior's command and formed by divine teaching, we dare to say:"
- **All:** "Our Father, who art in heaven, hallowed be thy name; thy kingdom come, thy will be done on earth as it is in heaven. Give us this day our daily bread, and forgive us our trespasses, as we forgive those who trespass against us; and lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil."

### 19. Embolism and Doxology
- **Priest:** "Deliver us, Lord, we pray, from every evil, graciously grant peace in our days, that, by the help of your mercy, we may be always free from sin and safe from all distress, as we await the blessed hope and the coming of our Savior, Jesus Christ."
- **All:** "For the kingdom, the power and the glory are yours now and for ever."

### 20. Sign of Peace
- **Priest:** "Lord Jesus Christ, who said to your Apostles: Peace I leave you, my peace I give you; look not on our sins, but on the faith of your Church, and graciously grant her peace and unity in accordance with your will. Who live and reign for ever and ever."
- **People:** "Amen."
- **Priest:** "The Peace of the Lord be with you always."
- **People:** "And with your spirit."
- **Deacon or Priest:** "Let us offer each other the sign of peace."
- **People** exchange a sign of peace (handshake, bow, etc.)

### 21. Fraction of the Bread (Lamb of God)
- **Priest breaks the host**
- **All:** "Lamb of God, you take away the sins of the world, have mercy on us." (twice)
- **All:** "Lamb of God, you take away the sins of the world, grant us peace."
- **Priest (quietly):** "May this mingling of the Body and Blood of our Lord Jesus Christ bring eternal life to those who receive it."

### 22. Communion
- **Priest holds host:** "Behold the Lamb of God, behold him who takes away the sins of the world. Blessed are those called to the supper of the Lamb."
- **All:** "Lord, I am not worthy that you should enter under my roof, but only say the word and my soul shall be healed."
- **People approach** for Communion
- **Minister:** "The Body of Christ." / "The Blood of Christ."
- **Communicant:** "Amen."
- **Communion Song** is sung during distribution

### 23. Prayer after Communion
- **Priest:** "Let us pray."
- **Priest** prays the Prayer after Communion (varies by day)
- **People:** "Amen."

---

## IV. CONCLUDING RITES

### 24. Announcements (if any)
- **Deacon or Priest** may make announcements

### 25. Final Blessing
- **Priest:** "The Lord be with you."
- **People:** "And with your spirit."
- **Priest** gives the blessing (simple or solemn formula)
- **People:** "Amen."

### 26. Dismissal
- **Deacon or Priest** (choose one):
  - "Go forth, the Mass is ended." / "Thanks be to God."
  - OR "Go and announce the Gospel of the Lord." / "Thanks be to God."
  - OR "Go in peace, glorifying the Lord by your life." / "Thanks be to God."
  - OR "Go in peace." / "Thanks be to God."

### 27. Recessional
- **Priest and ministers** exit in procession
- **Recessional hymn** may be sung

## NOTES ON VARIATIONS

### Optional Elements
- **Gloria**: Omitted during Advent and Lent; required on Sundays, Solemnities, and Feasts
- **Creed**: Required on Sundays and Solemnities; optional on other days
- **Second Reading**: Only on Sundays and Solemnities
- **Penitential Act**: May be omitted on Sundays when the Asperges (sprinkling with holy water) is used

### Seasonal Variations
- **Lent**: "Alleluia" is replaced; Gloria is omitted
- **Advent**: Gloria is omitted
- **Easter Season**: First Reading often from Acts of the Apostles

### Special Mass Types
- **Nuptial Mass**: Includes special blessings for marriage
- **Funeral Mass**: Includes special prayers for the deceased
- **Mass with Children**: May use Apostles' Creed and simplified language
- **Votive Masses**: For specific intentions or saints

### Liturgical Roles
- **Priest Celebrant**: Presides over the Mass
- **Deacon**: Assists priest, proclaims Gospel, gives homily (if no priest), gives dismissals
- **Concelebrants**: Other priests who concelebrate
- **Lectors**: Proclaim First and Second Readings
- **Cantor**: Leads singing, psalm responses
- **Altar Servers**: Assist at the altar
- **Extraordinary Ministers of Holy Communion**: Distribute Communion when needed

---

## STRUCTURAL SUMMARY

The Mass consists of two main parts that are closely connected:

1. **Liturgy of the Word**: Readings, homily, prayers - God speaks to His people
2. **Liturgy of the Eucharist**: Presentation of gifts, consecration, Communion - people offer the sacrifice and receive Christ

The Introductory Rites prepare us to enter into these sacred mysteries, and the Concluding Rites send us forth to live what we have celebrated.
